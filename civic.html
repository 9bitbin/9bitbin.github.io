Civic Engagement Resource Guide

Being non-partisan means not supporting any specific political party or candidate. Instead, it focuses on informing and empowering individuals to participate in democracy based on their own values and knowledge.

  • Farmingdale State College Office of Community and Civic Engagement (OCCE)
  • Island Harvest Food Bank
  • Red Cross of Greater NY
  • Volunteer New York!

Ways to participate in civic engagement at a local level:

  • Join a local community service group or advocacy organization.
  • Attend town hall meetings and voice your opinions.
  • Support local businesses that promote ethical practices.
  • Engage in campus-led volunteer initiatives.

Each year, Farmingdale State College's OCCE organizes a Day of Service, bringing students together to make an impact. Past efforts have included:

  • Community clean-up projects.
  • Food distribution at local shelters.
  • Voter registration drives.

According to CIRCLE at Tufts University:

  • Only 50% of eligible young voters (18-29 years old) voted in the 2020 election.
  • Gen Z and Millennials will make up nearly 40% of the electorate in 2024.
  • Youth voter turnout increased by 11% from 2016 to 2020.
