What is Civic Engagement?

Civic engagement for college students refers to actively participating in efforts that contribute to the betterment of their communities, both on and off campus.

  • Volunteering for local non-profits, shelters, or food drives.
  • Participating in student organizations focused on social causes.
  • Engaging in service-learning programs.
  • Advocating for social change through awareness campaigns.
  • Voting and political participation.
  • Interning with community-focused organizations.

Civic Engagement from a Global Perspective

  • Supporting global humanitarian efforts.
  • Engaging in international collaborations.
  • Participating in global activism.
  • Using technology and social media for advocacy.
  • Advancing democracy and civic rights worldwide.

Why is Voting Important for Students?

  • Influences policies affecting students.
  • Establishes lifelong civic engagement.
  • Amplifies youth voices.
  • Fulfills civic responsibility.
  • Shapes political agendas.
  • Enhances educational experience.
